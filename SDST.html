Welcome to SDST


Welcome to the San Diego School of Technology, a university located in San Diego, CA. Our schools main purpose is immerse and prepare students in STEM related fields. We do this through our world class education, which is collaborative and project-based, modeled after other universities such as the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, Stanford University, and Harvey Mudd College. Our History

In 1917, when San Diegos population was merely 18000 people, our founder, Simon Richards, moved to San Diego from San Francisco to excape the terrible fog. After spending five years in San Diego, he fell in love with the city. He wanted to give something back to San Diego, but he wasn't sure of what to give. Soon, his youngest son, Brilliam, brung up his wish for a school that focused on creating the future. Simon realized that this was the perfect idea to give back to the city. In five years, the original campus of SDST was built in Downtown, but after 15 years, the campus was moved to Clairemont and expanded there. In its opening year, SDST had 79 students and only offered one major, technology. Its safe to say the school has grown in the past 92 years.

As you can tell from our history, technology is imbedded into our university's identity. This wikipedia page gives an explanation of what technology is and its history. One thing that describes technology is that it's always changing to fit what society needs, and that is something that had been shown by SDST through its history. In 1940, SDST added two science related majors and began to allow students to choose a minor and a concentration. In 1953, SDST added our 7 majors relating to engineering. In 1970, our school's population became 2000. In the 1990's, SDST added a computer science major. Even as recently as 2010, SDST has made sure to change to allow their student the best experience, reducing class sizes despite an increasing school size. We have always worked to give our students what they need, even if it means change is necessary.
